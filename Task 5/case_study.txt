Title: The "Reverse-Request" UPI Scam
This case study looks at a common type of online payment fraud that targets college
students and young professionals. The scam usually starts when a student lists
something for sale on an online marketplace or receives a random call about a "refund"
or "scholarship".
The crime follows a specific step-by-step process. First, the attacker contacts the
victim, claiming they want to send money via a UPI app like GPay or PhonePe. Instead of
sending money, the attacker sends a "Request Money" link. They often pressure the
victim to act quickly, saying things like, "I've sent the payment, just click 'Approve' and
enter your PIN to receive it." This is where the trap is set—many people forget that you
never need to enter your UPI PIN to receive money, only to send it.
Once the victim enters their PIN, the money is instantly deducted from their bank
account and moved through multiple "mule" accounts to make it hard to track. The
consequences are often severe; for a student, losing a few thousand rupees can mean
losing their entire monthly allowance or semester fees. Even though individual losses
might seem small, the scale of these scams is massive, often funding larger criminal
networks
