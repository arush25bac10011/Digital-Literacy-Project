Researching this really opened my eyes to how simple psychological tricks are more dangerous than actual "hacking." I was surprised to find that the most successful scams don’t involve complex code—they just exploit a moment of distraction or a lack of knowledge about how apps like UPI actually work. One habit I am definitely changing as a result is how I handle random requests on my phone. I’ve started treating every "urgent" notification with a lot more skepticism, especially if it involves my bank account. I’m also making sure that my family knows about the "No PIN to receive" rule, as they might be even more vulnerable than I am. Being a Digital Ambassador means realizing that staying safe is more about building good habits than just using better software.
